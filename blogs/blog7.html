Neural Networks Explained: From Perceptrons to Deep Learning

Neural networks are the foundation of modern AI, driving advancements in image recognition, speech processing, and more. This blog explores their evolution and how they function.

What is a Neural Network?

A neural network is a computational system inspired by the human brain. It consists of layers of neurons that process and transform input data.

Types of Neural Networks

  • Perceptron: The simplest form, capable of basic binary classification.
  • Multilayer Perceptrons (MLPs): Multiple layers allow for complex decision-making.
  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs): Used for image processing.
  • Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs): Applied in sequential data tasks, like speech recognition.

Real-World Applications

  • Facial Recognition: Social media platforms use CNNs for face identification.
  • Voice Assistants: Alexa and Siri rely on RNNs for speech processing.
  • Medical Diagnosis: AI helps detect diseases from medical images.

Conclusion

Neural networks continue to evolve, pushing AI closer to human-like intelligence. Future advancements will further enhance their applications and impact across industries.
